Sod houses

An image of a sod house

Sod Houses were found all over the prairie because of the lack of trees. Sod is the top layer of dirt, it includes grass its roots, and the dirt that clings to the roots. Building these houses was no easy task. Especially if you did not have any neighbors to help you. One of the difficult aspects was cutting the sod. As the sod was cut a large tearing sound was heard. Glass for Windows was rare, and many had to use a candle to light up their house


Life on the prarie

An image of wagon used to migrate west

The labor shortage in the west eliminated many preconceptions about what women could and couldn't do. There were female lawyers, doctors, and real estate agents. For clothes many women made do with a gingham or calico dress. Many previously enslaved peoole tried to make home in the west
